All-vs-all correlation notebook

Which inputs correlate with each other?

A separate Colab notebook computes all-vs-all correlations across the catalog, with same-year Pearson/Spearman ranks, lag scans, module summaries, deduplicated cross-module browsing, and circular-shift placebo checks.

Current readout: 93 usable series and 4,030 same-year pairs. Raw leaders are mostly duplicate or same-construct measures such as population vs WDI population, so the notebook includes a deduplicated cross-module view for actual discovery.

Astrology correlation notebook

Do historical inputs correlate with astrology proxies?

A separate Colab notebook screens every catalog input against the exploratory astrology proxies, with same-year correlations, lag scans, family summaries, and circular-shift placebo checks.

Current readout: 528 same-year pairs were screened. The strongest raw pair was Uranus-Pluto hard-aspect proximity vs NAVCO injury proxy (r=0.637, n=23), but it did not beat its own circular-shift placebo at p<0.05. Seven of the top 25 raw pairs did beat circular-shift placebo and should be treated only as exploratory candidates.

Mechanism notebook

Solar-inflation mechanism exploration

A separate Colab notebook now tests the inflation finding with lag scans, placebo solar cycles, mechanism-candidate inputs, simple control models, rolling-origin validation, alternative inflation outcomes, and a revolution-mediation screen.

Current falsification readout: the solar-inflation hypothesis is substantially weakened. SILSO remains the strongest one-input predictor of Shiller annual inflation and beats shifted/synthetic placebo cycles, but it fails to generalize to non-Shiller inflation outcomes and is unstable under rolling-origin validation.
